The Social Progress Index (SPI)

Measures social progress on three broad dimensions: basic human needs, foundations of well-being, and opportunity.


The best way to improve happiness is by focusing political/economic resources towards factors driving forward social progress (identified by Social Progress Index). The reason Nordic countries are happier than America is because they focus policy on strengthening the social welfare of the state, and not GDP alone which doesn't account for any of these factors. For example, New Zealand has a “well-being budget” which budgets government spending around factors leading to well-being like robust social support systems focused around essentials like healthcare, housing, education, and a living wage necessary for the basic necessities of life. There’s an evident correlation between countries with high Social Progress Index scores and World Happiness Index scores. The most impactful ways to increase happiness scores of citizens derives from investing in institutions and programs that advance social welfare, environmental quality, and education. If we prioritize and measure these things, then we will be more likely to see progress on multiple fronts. Focusing solely on GDP is unduly dominating our attention and resources when there are other factors and multi-faceted approaches that can substantially better increase happiness and well-being. At the very least we should try to rework criteria for GDP to include measures like health, life expectancy, and environmental quality.

Why This Matters

1. Happier people are more likely to volunteer in the community and donate money to charity.

2. Economic voting evident on individual and national level: individuals with healthy financial situations are more likely to prefer governing parties, and incumbent parties receive higher vote shares with a better election year economy.
